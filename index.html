Reading notes

  • Proxies are dashed lines. A PPI proxy is a US producer-price index tracking the cost family, not a quoted market price for your grade — use it for direction and timing, not for absolute price checks.
  • Plastics are China domestic spot (CNY/t) from SunSirs — closest to what your China-side suppliers actually pay. The Mar 2026 polymer spike is real (mirrored, lagged, in the US PPI).
  • Freight "Sweden" = China→North Europe base ports. The 20ft Europe line is genuine per-TEU data (SCFI); its Aug–Nov 2025 gap is a data-availability gap, deliberately left broken rather than interpolated. FBX free history is irregular (weekly blog posts) — it densifies as weekly refreshes accumulate.
  • Femcare materials share series across the board: the airlaid/fluff and absorbent-core pulp track the Wood pulp line, release/wrapper papers track Packaging paper, the bioplastic back film tracks PLA, and inbound ocean freight is tracked via the China lanes as a directional proxy for the India→US/EU routes actually used. SAP, PBAT and hot-melt have no free quoted price — they ride labeled proxies (Brent feedstock chain, adhesives PPI).
  • FRED/PPI series lag ~1–2 months (latest observation May 2026 as of the 2026-07-08 build); recent PPI months are preliminary and can be revised.
  • Every value on this page was fetched from its named source on the retrieval date recorded in the embedded database — no interpolated or estimated points anywhere.
